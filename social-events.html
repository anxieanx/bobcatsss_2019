Excursions

Museum of Slavonia

It was founded in 1877 under the name “Museum of the free and royal city of Osijek”. Over the decades it grew into a complex, multidisciplinary museum (focussed on the region of eastern Croatia called Slavonia). Today it houses almost four thousand registered artefacts divided into nine departments such as Natural history, Archaeology (Prehistory, Antique, great migrations, Medieval), Numismatics, History, Arts and crafts, Ethnology, Technical. Amongst its numerous collections some of the most prized ones are The Roman Mursa collection and numismatic collections. Museum also has a library with a collection of more than 70 000 books, many of them from an early printing period. The two baroque buildings that house the museum are also venues for many concerts and art exhibitions. Visit the Web Page

Museum of Water Life

This collection is a unique exhibition of seashells and marine and freshwater life. It is one of the biggest collections of this type in Central Europe and contains some of the rarest exhibits. As Lonely Planet says „Check out the most poisonous creature in the ocean (the remains of an octopus from the Philippines), fossils from 650 million years ago, a megalodon tooth and a vast array of exotic shells.” Visit the Facebook Page

Osijek Walking Tour

Osijek is also known as "City on the Drava River" and was named after the word "oseka" which means „low tide“ (a place near the river suited for inhabitants). It's the fourth biggest city in Croatia with a population of about 100,000 and is the cultural, economic and scientific centre of Slavonia and Baranja region. Osijek is located close to the confluence of the Drava and the Danube, in the middle of its 2850 km long course from German Schwarzwald to the Black Sea. In the city Croatian and old Austro-Hungarian heritage meets which is evidenced in the architecture, rich traditions and gastronomy. (Tourist Board of the City of Osijek). We will take you on a walking tour where you will be able to see many of the city sights and hear about Osijek's history and present. Find more information at City Sights

City tour is included in the registration fee.

Visit will be scheduled for Monday (21st of January) and Tuesday (22nd of January) at about 4 p.m.

Osijek Cinematographs

You will be able to see the show with few short movies directed by awarded Croatian directors. The show will be held in cinema Urania built in 1920. The building was built in Art Nouveau style as a cinema and Masonic temple and has a triangular facade. Across the cinema you can find two statues of sphinxes that guard the temple. Urania Cinema was awarded a prestigeous 2018 Europa Cinemas awards (Best Young Audience Activities).

Show will be scheduled for Wednesday at 5 p.m.

Price: ca 5 Euros (please prepare Croatian kuna in cash).
